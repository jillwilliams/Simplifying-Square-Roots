Simplifying Square Roots

Sometimes it is necessary to simplify a square root without using a calculator. We call this simplifying a square root by hand.
To simplify a square root by hand there are two steps.
  1. Factor the radicand (the number under the square root sign) by using a factor tree.
  2. Then look for repeating pairs of square roots in order to simplify the expression.

Rule: The square root of any number, squared, equals the radicand.
Look for pairs of square roots to simplify using the formula:
√a ∙ √a = a.

Notice the similarity between a factor tree and simplifying square roots. Each time you simplify a square root you will need to utilize the factor tree to make life easier. For every pair of factors, simplify. Combine all remaining numbers by multiplication and this value will be the new radicand.
